About

AI for work that
doesn't scale without it

I'm not interested in using AI for things I could do just as well myself in the same time. What genuinely excites me is the category of work that simply wouldn't happen otherwise — tasks too large, too repetitive, or too complex to tackle manually at any useful scale.

My approach starts with experimentation: testing what's actually possible, finding where AI adds real leverage. But experimentation without follow-through is just a hobby. What I value is turning those discoveries into repeatable workflows — solutions that keep delivering value without ongoing effort.

⟳
Scale over speed
AI is most powerful when it removes the ceiling on what's possible — not just when it makes existing tasks faster.
⬡
Experiment, then systematise
Good instincts about what AI can do come from trying things. But the real value is in building something repeatable from what works.
◎
Judgment stays human
AI handles the volume. I handle the thinking about whether the output is actually right, useful, and worth acting on.

Automation · Content
Brand-trained translation agent in Microsoft Copilot

Built a Copilot agent for content editors that translates copy into other languages — trained on our brand language, tone, and terminology so the output reads like us, not like a machine.

Problem
Translations done through generic tools produced technically correct copy that felt off-brand — wrong tone, missing terminology, inconsistent style. Editors had to rewrite heavily after every pass.
Approach
Built a custom Copilot agent with a detailed system prompt covering our brand voice, preferred vocabulary, terms to avoid, and style conventions in the target language. Trained and iterated the agent with content editors, incorporating their corrections until output matched brand standard. Then ran a training session so editors could use and refine it independently.
Outcome
Content editors can now produce on-brand translations without external agencies or heavy post-editing. The agent handles the linguistic heavy lifting; editors focus on final review and nuance — a task that previously required specialist involvement now runs in-house at scale.
Result
In-house brand-accurate translation at scale, editors fully independent
Tools
Microsoft Copilot · Copilot Studio
Analysis · Training
AI visibility benchmarking with HubSpot AEO

Used HubSpot's AEO tool to evaluate how our business appears across ChatGPT, Gemini, and Perplexity compared to competitors — then translated those findings into a practical training session for content editors.

Problem
No visibility into how our brand was being represented in LLMs — or whether competitors were outperforming us in AI-generated answers where buyers now do early research.
Approach
Ran the AEO tool across our brand and key competitors, analysing visibility scores, sentiment, share of voice, and which content was being cited. Used the gap analysis to build a structured training session teaching content editors how AI answer engines work and what content changes improve citation rates.
Outcome
Clear competitive benchmark established. Content team equipped with actionable AEO principles — direct answers, structured headers, consistent entity coverage — applicable immediately to existing and new content.
Result
Benchmark established + team trained on AEO content principles
Tools
HubSpot AEO · ChatGPT · Gemini · Perplexity

How I write prompts that work

A good prompt isn't a question — it's a brief. Here's the anatomy of how I structure prompts for reliable, scalable output.

Prompt anatomy

Each layer adds precision. Hover a block to see why it matters.

01
Role & Context
"You are an experienced data analyst reviewing a financial summary for a non-technical audience…"
02
Task
"Identify the three most significant trends and explain each in plain language."
03
Constraints
"Do not use jargon. Keep each explanation under 40 words. Avoid hedging language."
04
Output format
"Return as a numbered list. Bold the trend name, then the explanation on the next line."
05
Example (optional)
"Example: **Revenue growth** — Income increased 18% YoY, driven by new enterprise contracts."

Summarisation
Meeting notes → action list
Turns raw, unstructured meeting notes into a clean list of owners, actions, and deadlines — ready to paste into a project tracker.
Prompt
You are a project coordinator. I will give you raw meeting notes. Extract every action item mentioned. For each, identify: (1) the owner if named, (2) the task in one sentence, (3) the deadline if stated. Format as a table with columns: Owner | Action | Deadline. If any field is unclear, write "TBC". Do not include discussion points — only commitments.
Why it works
The role primes for structured extraction. "TBC" handles ambiguity without hallucination. "Do not include discussion points" prevents output bloat.
